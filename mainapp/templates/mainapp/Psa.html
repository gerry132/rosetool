Personal Skills Audit


Introduction

Currently I work as a Data Analyst and although I don’t hate my work it is not something I am passionate about. About a year ago I made the decision to once again return to further education and this time to follow my dreams of working in the creative industry. Since an early age I loved drawing and painting and this followed me throughout my formative years. Sadly upon leaving secondary school I decided not to pursue Art & Design this decision was informed by many factors at the time and too much to try to explain but I here I am now trying to fulfil the dreams of my teenage self. I went into this course not really knowing what to expect and also I wasn’t really clear on what avenue of graphic design I wanted to pursue. In reality I sent my expectations low for myself as I didn’t want to be disappointed on perhaps not enjoying it or have the capacity or skill to apply myself to the requirements of the course. One of the main driving forces behind my decision to apply for this course was my new found interest in Photoshop. About 2 years ago I reignited my interest in Photography and started an Instagram account over time I learned of free photo editing apps and used them to modify my images and really enjoyed it and gain quite a few followers.

Conclusion

Upon research possible career paths that would incorporate my Computer Science knowledge and Graphic Design skills I found that possibly a role as either a UI or UX designer would suit me. To gain a better understanding of the requirements of the role I searched indeed.ie. I found that there were numerous roles available most were more senior but a graduate one looked less intimidating. Surprisingly I do have many of the skills required already but perhaps not the duration in practice. Here is a screenshot of the job description: During my course in Computer Science I designed a mobile news app and a ecommerce website so I would evaluate myself as having basic to intermediate knowledge of HTML, CSS, Bootstrap and Javascript. While designing my website I used Bootstrap to regulate the responsiveness of the pages to different interfaces. I also studied Sociology and Anthropology and have strong research skills particularly into human behaviour and psychology which can be useful in a role such as this.

I decided to do some additional research into UX and wanted to differentiate it between UI to decide which best applied to me. According to Raffaela Rein:

“there are skills that are unique to each role. UX designers typically have experience with organizing and executing research, wireframing, prototyping, information architecture, interaction design, coding, and analytics. UI designers usually have experience with responsive visual interface design, layouts and grid systems, visual hierarchy, typography, color theory, animation, accessibility, and A/B testing.” (2018)

I then decided to look into UI design as it seems to appeal to me more as despite knowing a bit about coding I would prefer a more visual geared career.

In summary I found this analysis very helpful in assessing the skills I possess and what I need to develop. I also got a good idea what roles I could pursue with the skillset I currently have. Although I would be well suited to web development of UX design I would still like to consider other roles perhaps those that offer a variety in the work I would do. I realise that I may fear pursing a role such as creative director or art director as I doubt my ability to lead a team or overcome my fear of public speaking. I don't want tnhis fear to hold me back all my life and probably the most important skill I would need to work on is this and hopefully it will no longer stand in my way.
