Week 3: Bauhaus

The Bauhaus school was founded by Walter Gropius in Weimar in 1919. His objective was to unify art with industrial technology "they encouraged the usage of modern technologies and believed thatforms follow function and that the artist and the craftsman should be united in one individual, and focued on the productivity instead of the mere beauty of the design"

After the school closed in 1933 due to pressure from the Naxi Regime many of its educators and students spread across the globe bringing with them the aesthetics and philosophy of the Bauhaus school and what it introduced to the world was a new way of viewing objects and a different conception of design.

The Bauhaus school was the first to establisha dedicated Typography department. Educators at the school emphasised the importance of using the grid system and recommended the use of san serif fonts due to their clean uncomplicated design. From these teachings designers began to organise information differently using the grid and this is still evident today in many designs and a good example of its practice is in web design. Web design has totally embraced the uncomplicated and clean principles of Buahaus this is seen in many examples such as Microsoft.

In 2010 Microsoft decided to adopt what is called a flat design influenced by the Bauhaus movement. This visual scheme is ubiquitous across all of Microsoft's products including the Windows phone, XBOX, and Widows OS. "Now when you look at a Windows Phone, Windows 8 tablet or PC, or an Xbox console, they look like a family of devices. And when I use these devices, they behave like a family of devices, sharing my content, settings and experiences across them. " He goes on to say " When Sam Moreau, design director for Windows, says, “The content is the interface,” he’s channeling his inner Bauhaus. "
