April Greiman’s impact on design and other creative industries

April Greiman was one of the first graphic designers to embrace computer technology as a medium for creating art, nowadays graphic designers see a computer as an indespinsible tool but many of her contemporary's feared the increasing amount of digitalization

"This fascination comes from the core of her being, a core of perpetual curiosity and questioning that fuels her desire to explore and inspires the cutting-edge design work that places her at the helm of integrated design at the close of the twentieth century" (Heller, 1998)

Influences

She describes her style as a blend of "...technology, science, word and image with color and space..." (Greiman, 2020)

Greiman studied at the Basel School of Design in Switzerland, it was there that she was introduced to the New Wave style by Wolfgang Weingart who had been teaching his students his this technique since the 1970's. The New Wave style was heavily influenced by Punk culture and the Swiss style and took many elements that characterised the Swiss style and distorted and warped them. Letter spacing became widened and the consistency of font style, sizes and orientation varied even within a word in essence breaking all the fundamental rules of Typography single word.


Cultural Context

April Greiman was one of the first to embrace the computer as a tool to create artistic images and helped create the new-wave post-modern design movement. In 1975 the first personal desktop was introduced and when the Apple Mac emerged in 1984 it offered consumers the first graphical interface. It was around this period that Greiman started using the Apple Macintosh in her work. Before in the rise in popularity in desktop based tools every facet of a design was completed by hand by a graphic designer, today Photoshop and Illustrator are essential to the role.

Comparative Analysis

Technology is advancing at an exponential rate and with this artists have embraced these new tools in new and innovative waves.

Nowadays we take the digital tools we use for granted but when April Greiman she only had a 1 Megabyte computer at hand with a monochrome 9 inch display. The process was so slow that she had to send the files to print before she left her studio to have it ready for the morning. (Dill, 2020)
